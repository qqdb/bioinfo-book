非编码 RNA 类型及介绍

Introduction

As a field of study, bioinformatics has revolutionized the way we understand and execute biological processes. One of its major areas of focus is the study of non-coding RNAs (ncRNA), which has opened new dimensions in the field of molecular biology, genetics, and biochemistry. In this article, we will explore one of the most important and fascinating classes of ncRNAs - the non-coding RNA types, their functions, and significance.

Non-Coding RNA (ncRNA)

RNA is a central molecule in the DNA-to-protein central dogma. Traditionally, RNA is considered as a messenger with the primary role of transmitting genetic information from DNA to ribosomes for protein synthesis. However, recent research has revealed that not all RNA molecules code for proteins. In fact, only about 2% of the human genome is dedicated to encoding proteins. The remaining 98% of RNA molecules are non-coding RNAs, which are molecules that do not give rise to proteins but play important regulatory roles in the cell.

There are several types of non-coding RNAs, such as transfer RNA (tRNA), ribosomal RNA (rRNA), small nuclear RNA (snRNA), small nucleolar RNA (snoRNA), and microRNA (miRNA). This article will focus on the most exciting class of ncRNAs - the ones that do not encode for proteins and the functions they perform in the cell.

Long Non-Coding RNA (lncRNA)

Long non-coding RNAs (lncRNAs) are a class of ncRNAs that are more than 200 nucleotides in length. These molecules are mainly transcribed by RNA polymerase II and processed similarly to protein-coding genes. LncRNAs are different from protein-coding genes in that they do not have an open reading frame (ORF), and hence do not encode for proteins.

The functions of lncRNAs are diverse, ranging from regulating gene expression, chromatin modification, and epigenetic processes. LncRNAs have also been found to be involved in a wide range of biological processes, such as cell differentiation, embryonic development, and immune regulation. Some lncRNAs have also been demonstrated to play important roles in various diseases, including cancer.

Circular RNA (circRNA)

Circular RNAs (circRNAs) are a class of ncRNAs whose ends are covalently linked, forming a closed loop structure. Unlike linear RNAs, circRNAs are resistant to exonucleases and are stable, making them an ideal biomarker for diagnosis and treatment of many diseases.

CircRNAs have been found to act as miRNA sponges, regulating gene expression and contributing to various biological processes, such as cell proliferation, differentiation, and apoptosis. Some circRNAs have also been linked to several diseases, including cancer, cardiovascular diseases, and neurological disorders.

MicroRNA (miRNA)

MicroRNAs (miRNAs) are a class of small ncRNA molecules that regulate gene expression post-transcriptionally. They are transcribed from DNA, processed into mature miRNAs, and then bind to messenger RNAs (mRNAs) to repress translation or trigger mRNA degradation.

MiRNAs are essential for diverse cellular functions, such as cell proliferation, differentiation, and apoptosis. Disregulation of miRNA expression has been linked to many diseases, including cancer, cardiovascular diseases, and neurological disorders. Since miRNAs have been reported to play a significant role in gene expression regulation, they have been extensively studied for their underlying mechanisms and therapeutic potential.

Small Interfering RNA (siRNA)

Small interfering RNAs (siRNAs) are a class of small RNA molecules that regulate gene expression post-transcriptionally by binding to complementary mRNA sequences and triggering their degradation. The mechanism of action of siRNAs is similar to that of miRNAs. However, siRNAs are typically created artificially in the lab and used for gene knockdown experiments or treating genetic disorders.

Conclusion

In conclusion, non-coding RNAs are crucial components of regulatory networks that control gene expression and play important roles in various biological processes. The study of ncRNAs has led to significant progress in the understanding of disease mechanisms and has great potential in the field of therapeutics. While much is already known about ncRNAs, there is still much to uncover about the role of these molecules in regulating gene expression and the broader biological processes that they influence.
